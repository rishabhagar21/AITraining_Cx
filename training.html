Purpose of This Presentation

This briefing presents a structured 30-60-90 day AI enablement roadmap for the CX SP organization. Our goal is to convert the team's already-high AI adoption into measurable, role-based capability through a governed training program with clear leadership visibility, accountability, and decision points.

Why Now — The Business Case

  • Adoption is ahead of capability: 259 of 261 team members already use AI, with strong daily usage — yet only 108 are formally AI-trained, leaving a clear readiness gap.
  • Competitive imperative: AI fluency directly impacts delivery speed, automation quality, and customer outcomes across Network Engineering, PM, SDM, and Solution Architecture.
  • Governance & risk: A structured program ensures responsible, secure AI use and a consistent skill baseline across every CX role.

Why We Are Building This Table

The survey shows strong AI usage but weaker formal training completion. This table is used to track assignment, completion, reminders, escalation, and leadership visibility so adoption is converted into measurable capability.

Slide 1: Automated Assignment & Initial Tracking (Days 1-7)

  • Trigger & Kickoff: Upon curriculum publication, the platform dynamically assigns "MUST HAVE" modules based on the user's CX Role (Network Eng, PM, SDM, Sol Arch) with a strict 14-day due date.
  • Week 1 Gentle Nudge: On Day 7, if the user has not reached 100% completion, the platform automatically sends Reminder 1 directly to the individual to keep them on track.

Slide 2: Escalation & Leadership Reporting (Day 14+)

  • Due Date Escalation: On Day 14, if "MUST HAVE" modules remain incomplete, the platform sends Reminder 2 to the individual and CCs their Direct Manager to drive closure.
  • Bi-Weekly Visibility: The platform automatically generates and distributes a bi-weekly progress report to CX Leadership and Department Managers, tracking completion rates, overdue assignments, and voluntary enrollment in "GOOD TO HAVE" modules.
